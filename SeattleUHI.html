Pharmacy Accessibility in Iowa

Analyzing Access to Essential Pharmacy Services Across Communities

Project Overview

Pharmacies play a vital role in community health by providing access to medications and supporting preventive and ongoing care. This project examines pharmacy accessibility across Iowa, focusing on vulnerable populations and the interplay of socioeconomic conditions, population density, and transportation networks. The study evaluates disparities in access and identifies areas where interventions may be necessary.

Objectives

  • Assess spatial distribution of pharmacies across Iowa.
  • Identify communities with limited access to essential pharmacy services.
  • Examine correlations between pharmacy access, socioeconomic status, and population density.
  • Evaluate transportation networks and their influence on access for vulnerable groups.

Data & Methods

Pharmacy locations, census demographic data, and transportation network information were compiled in a GIS environment. Spatial analysis included calculating travel distances, accessibility indices, and mapping areas with limited service. Socioeconomic overlays were used to identify disparities in access among vulnerable populations.

Key Findings

  • Rural areas, particularly in western Iowa, have the lowest pharmacy accessibility.
  • Socioeconomic factors strongly influence access to pharmacies, with higher-income areas generally having better service.
  • Population density is positively correlated with pharmacy availability, highlighting urban-rural disparities.
  • Transportation limitations exacerbate access challenges for vulnerable populations in remote communities.
