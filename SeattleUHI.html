Urban Heat Island in Seattle

Spatial Analysis of Temperature Variation and Urban Land Cover

Project Overview

This project investigates the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect in Seattle by analyzing spatial temperature patterns in relation to land cover types. The study examines how urban density, impervious surfaces, vegetation cover, and proximity to water influence localized temperature variation.

Objectives

  • Map spatial distribution of surface temperature across Seattle.
  • Identify hotspots associated with high-density urban development.
  • Compare temperature variation with vegetation and impervious surface data.
  • Evaluate mitigation opportunities through urban greening strategies.

Data & Methods

Satellite-derived temperature data was processed and analyzed in GIS. Land cover classifications were used to differentiate vegetation, built-up areas, and water bodies. Spatial overlays and zonal statistics were applied to evaluate correlations between surface temperature and land cover characteristics.

Key Findings

  • Downtown Seattle exhibits the strongest UHI intensity.
  • Areas with dense vegetation show significantly lower surface temperatures.
  • Water bodies moderate nearby temperature extremes.
  • Impervious surface concentration strongly correlates with elevated heat zones.
