Methods for Reducing Urban Heat Islands

A comparative analysis of urban heat island mitigation strategies in Seattle, El Paso, Jackson, and Norfolk using land surface temperature and land use data.

Project Overview

This project examines urban heat island (UHI) mitigation strategies across four U.S. cities: Seattle, El Paso, Jackson, and Norfolk. Each city represents a different climate, urban form, and policy context.

Using land surface temperature data and land use classifications, the analysis evaluates how factors such as green infrastructure, urban density, and surface materials influence temperature patterns and cooling effectiveness.
