About Me

Hi! I'm Bella a Geography (GIS Track) student at the University of Iowa with a passion for turning spatial data into real-world insight. I love connecting environmental science, public policy, and geospatial analysis to understand how people interact with their landscapes.

My Background

I’m originally from Cedar Rapids, Iowa, and I’ve always loved maps, landscapes, and exploring how the environment shapes our daily lives. When I discovered GIS, it immediately clicked! It blends analytical thinking, creativity, and problem-solving in a way that feels purposeful.

Throughout my time at Iowa, I’ve built experience across geospatial tools, fieldwork, and data analysis in both environmental and social contexts.

What I Study

My academic work focuses on:

  • LiDAR & terrain modeling — DEM/DSM creation, hydrology tools, surface analysis
  • Spatial databases — PostGIS, schema design, SQL querying, ER diagrams
  • Environmental analysis — flood risk, land cover, soils, conservation
  • Public health mapping — childhood cancer, environmental exposure, spatial statistics
  • Cartography — making clear, intuitive maps that communicate meaningfully

Experience

I’ve worked on a variety of projects through coursework and internships, including:

  • Modeling flood-prone areas in downtown Cedar Rapids using LiDAR & hydrology workflows
  • Building a spatial database combining trails, parks, and demographic data
  • Analyzing agricultural runoff and childhood cancer risk across Iowa counties
  • Creating GIS-based conservation research on Laysan Island seabird habitats
  • Applying regression and GWR models to explore health & environment relationships

Beyond GIS

Outside of academics, I love weight-lifting, gaming, yoga, hiking, and writing. I’m excited about careers that combine environmental science, spatial analytics, and real-world problem-solving, whether in government, environmental consulting, public health, or GIS engineering.
