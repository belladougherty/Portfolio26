Mapping Noise Pollution in U.S. Cities

Noise pollution mapping and its health impacts in U.S. cities, with a focus on high-noise areas like industrial zones and highways.

Project Overview

In this project, I focused on mapping noise pollution levels across U.S. cities, particularly in areas with high levels of industrial activity or heavy traffic such as highways and industrial zones.

The goal was to analyze the relationship between noise pollution and its impact on public health, particularly in terms of respiratory issues, sleep disturbances, and other health concerns linked to noise exposure. The analysis used noise data, health data, and GIS tools to identify noise pollution hotspots and assess their health impacts.

Tools used in the analysis include GIS platforms like ArcGIS and QGIS, with a focus on overlaying noise data with health statistics to visualize the potential health risks associated with noise exposure.

Analysis Results

Below are the key findings of the noise pollution analysis, including maps and visualizations that demonstrate noise pollution patterns and their potential health impacts in urban areas.

Noise Pollution Map

This map displays noise pollution levels across various U.S. cities, focusing on high-noise areas such as highways and industrial zones.

Health Impacts

This map highlights areas with high noise pollution levels and overlays health data, such as respiratory issues and sleep disturbances, to identify vulnerable populations.

Conclusions & Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following conclusions and recommendations were made:

  • Areas with high levels of noise pollution, especially in industrial and heavily trafficked zones, show a correlation with higher rates of respiratory issues and sleep disturbances.
  • It is recommended to implement noise mitigation measures, such as sound barriers along highways and stricter noise regulations in industrial zones.
  • Local authorities should consider using noise reduction strategies as part of urban planning to protect public health, especially in high-risk areas.

These findings aim to guide city planners and policymakers in addressing the health impacts of noise pollution and improving the overall quality of life in urban environments.
