Shake Shack’s Network Analysis in NYC

Network analysis of Shake Shack locations in New York City, assessing which stores should close based on service areas, demographics, and median income.

Project Overview

In this project, I performed a network analysis of Shake Shack locations in New York City, assessing the impact of location factors such as service area coverage, nearby demographics, and median income to recommend which stores could be closed or relocated.

Tools used in the analysis include ArcGIS and QGIS for spatial analysis and mapping. The project aimed to provide actionable insights for better network optimization and decision-making.

Conclusions & Recommendations

Based on the analysis, we can conclude that certain Shake Shack locations may be underperforming due to factors like low population density or demographic mismatches. The following recommendations were made:

  • Relocate the lower-performing locations to more affluent or higher-traffic areas.
  • Consolidate service areas to ensure optimal market coverage without over-saturation.
  • Explore new market segments based on income distribution and age trends.

These findings aim to enhance Shake Shack’s network strategy for more efficient growth and customer outreach.
